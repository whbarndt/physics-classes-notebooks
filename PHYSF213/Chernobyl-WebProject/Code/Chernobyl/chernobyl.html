The nuclear reactor personnel weren't able to continue the shutdown procedure until about 8 hours later. At this point they proceeded with the shutdown as usual but with a newly appointed night shift who had no news of this test. As they were bringing the power down to 700 MW, for non-concretely known reasons, the thermal power inside the reactor dropped to 30MW. Normal procedure at this point would be to either shutdown the reactor or slowly raise the thermal energy over the course of a day, however, the lead engineer said to proceed with the test. To do so, they started taking control rods out. They brought it up to 200 MW and decided to do the test. At this moment when the water pumps were taken offline, the reactor core's thermal energy instantly began to rise. In an attempt to stop the power surge they pressed a fail-safe button called AZ-5 which inserted every control rod back into the reactor core. However, unknowing to the personnel, these rods that had graphite near the ends enhanced the thermal energy at the bottom of the core to the point where the thermal power was greater than the design limit. Which then caused an initial explosion, from the built up steam, followed by a second explosion when the core ignites from the incoming oxygen.

This was the tragedy of Chernobyl.



On April 26, 1986 in the north of the Ukranian SSR, Reactor 4 of the Chernobyl (or Chornobyl in Ukraine) Power Plant unexpectedly exploded. It is considered the worst nuclear disaster in history and the only event in history that resulted in fatalities having to do with commercial nuclear power. It is estimated to have killed at least 60 people but possibly more due to the radiation that escaped.

On that day there was supposed to be a routine safety test for the reactor (how ironic). This test consisted of bringing down the thermal power inside the reactor core to 700M MW (Mega Watts) to simulate a power outage to check and see if the remaining rotational power from the turbines would keep water circulation running until the back up electrical generators could start.

Normally the reactors thermal energy runs at about 3,200 MW. Earlier in the day they started the shutdown, slowly bringing down the energy. However, as that was happening the electrical dispatcher halted the shutdown, holding the  thermal energy at about 1,600 MW.

This is the start of what went wrong.
