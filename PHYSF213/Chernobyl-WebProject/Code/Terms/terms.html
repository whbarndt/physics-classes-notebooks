Terms and Definitions:
  • Proton: A positively charged elementary particle (Little yellow guys in photo)
  • Neutron: A neutrally charged elementary particle (Little orange guys in photo)
  • Nucleus: The center of the atom, primarily composed of neutrons and protons.
  • Element: An element is a substance that has the same number of protons.
(For example: Hydrogen, Oxygen, Carbon, Uranium are all elements)
  • Isotope: A variant of a particular element that differs in the number of neutrons.
  • Unstable Isotope: A type of isotope that is radioactive.
  • Radioactive or Radioactive decay: Is when an unstable nucleus loses energy 
by emitted radiation.
  • Half-Life: It's the amount of time for one-half of a radioactive isotope to decay.
  • Primary types of nuclear reactions: Radioactive Decay, Fusion, and Fission
